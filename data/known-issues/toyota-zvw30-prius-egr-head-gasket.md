# Toyota ZVW30 Prius — EGR clogging and head gasket failure

**English** | [日本語](./toyota-zvw30-prius-egr-head-gasket.ja.md)

## Scope

- **Vehicles:** Toyota Prius ZVW30 (3rd generation, 2009–2015); the same 2ZR-FXE powertrain in Prius v/α (ZVW40/41), Lexus CT200h, and early Prius PHV shares the mechanism at lower reported rates
- **Engines/components:** 2ZR-FXE 1.8L Atkinson-cycle four — EGR cooler, EGR valve, intake manifold EGR passages, cylinder head gasket
- **Production window:** 2009–2015 production; higher-mileage cars (roughly 150,000 km+) are where failures concentrate
- **Markets:** Global; heavily documented in both Japan and North America

## The issue

The 2ZR-FXE recirculates a large volume of exhaust gas for efficiency. Over time carbon progressively clogs the EGR cooler, valve, and the individual EGR ports in the intake manifold. Clogging is uneven across cylinders, leaning some cylinders and raising combustion stress — the widely observed end result is a blown head gasket, often first noticeable as a cold-start misfire when coolant seeps into a cylinder overnight. Hybrid engine stop/start cycling means many more cold-ish restarts than a conventional car, which amplifies the symptom.

## Symptoms an owner can detect

- Knock/rattle and rough shake for 1–2 seconds when the engine first fires after sitting overnight, clearing as it runs — the classic early sign
- Misfire codes P0301–P0304 (often P0301/P0302) plus P0A80-adjacent hybrid codes in advanced cases
- Unexplained slow coolant loss with no visible leak
- White exhaust smoke on cold start in advanced cases
- Worsening fuel economy and EGR-flow codes (P0401) from the clogging itself, sometimes years before gasket failure

## Severity

**Major.** The car usually remains drivable in early stages, but continued driving pumps coolant into a cylinder; the proper repair is a head gasket job or engine replacement, and unaddressed cases can hydrolock or overheat the engine.

## Frequency (honest assessment)

EGR/intake clogging is essentially universal with mileage on this engine; progression to head gasket failure is common enough at 150,000–250,000 km that hybrid-specialist shops treat it as the defining ZVW30 failure, but it is not a "every car by 100,000 km" event. Preventive EGR/intake cleaning is widely believed (owner-community and shop consensus, not a Toyota-published fix) to reduce the risk.

## Fix and cost

- **Repair:** Head gasket replacement with head inspection/resurface, plus full EGR circuit and intake manifold cleaning (skipping the cleaning invites a repeat). Alternatives: used or rebuilt engine swap. Preventive: EGR cooler/valve and intake port cleaning around every 80,000–100,000 km
- **Typical cost:** Head gasket job JPY 15–30万円 (Japan independents/hybrid specialists) / USD 1,500–3,000 (US); used engine swap similar or slightly more. Preventive EGR cleaning JPY 3–8万円 / USD 300–800

## Buyer checklist

- Cold-start the car yourself after it has sat overnight — a first-fire rattle/shudder that clears is the tell; a warm test drive hides it
- Check coolant reservoir level and ask about top-up history; look for head gasket or engine replacement records (documented repair with EGR cleaning is a plus)
- Ask whether EGR/intake cleaning has ever been done; on a 150,000 km+ car with no record, price the job in
- Scan for stored/pending misfire codes before purchase

## Sources

Extensively documented in Japanese and North American Prius owner communities and hybrid-specialist shops; Toyota TSBs cover EGR-related driveability on this engine family, but the head-gasket linkage is community/shop consensus rather than an official campaign. Verify each car individually.
