# Nissan (Jatco) CVT — Overheating, judder, and premature failure

**English** | [日本語](./nissan-jatco-cvt-failure.ja.md)

## Scope

- **Vehicles:** Nissan Altima, Rogue, Sentra, Versa, Murano, Pathfinder (2013+), X-Trail, Serena, Teana, Lafesta and other CVT-equipped Nissans; some Mitsubishi and Suzuki models use the same Jatco units
- **Engines/components:** Jatco CVT families — RE0F09A (3.5L V6), RE0F10A/JF011E (2.0–2.5L four), JF015E (CVT7, small cars), JF016E/JF017E (later 2.0–3.5L)
- **Production window:** Roughly 2003–2018, with the worst reputation concentrated in 2003–2010 and again in 2013–2016 (JF016E/JF015E early years); gradual improvements after
- **Markets:** Global. The formal warranty extension (doubling to 10 years/120,000 miles on 2003–2010 models) was a US program; Japan and other markets handled cases individually

## The issue

Jatco belt-and-pulley CVTs in this era are sensitive to heat and fluid condition. Fluid degradation and overheating lead to belt/pulley surface wear and valve-body/torque-converter judder; debris then circulates and finishes off bearings and the pulley faces. Once wear starts, the transmission typically progresses from shudder and whine to slipping, limp mode, and total failure. Repair is usually replacement rather than overhaul.

## Symptoms an owner can detect

- Shudder or judder on light acceleration around 20–60 km/h (feels like driving over rumble strips)
- A rising whine or grinding note that tracks vehicle speed, absent when stationary
- RPM flare (revs rise without matching acceleration) — belt slip
- Power reduction / limp mode on long highway climbs or hot days (fluid-temperature protection), recovering after cooling
- Scan tool: CVT fluid deterioration counter high; codes such as P0746, P17F0/P17F1 (judder detection on later units)

## Severity

**Major.** Failure disables the car but is not a direct safety catastrophe; the cost is a substantial fraction of an affected used car's value.

## Frequency (honest assessment)

Failure rates high enough that Nissan doubled the powertrain warranty for CVT on 2003–2010 US models and later extended coverage on several 2012–2017 models — but the majority of units still reach high mileage, especially with frequent fluid service. Highway-heavy, hot-climate, and towing use fail earlier. Judder complaints on JF015E/JF016E (Sentra, Versa, Note, Serena era) are common; catastrophic failure is less so.

## Fix and cost

- **Repair:** Valve-body or torque-converter replacement fixes some judder cases; worn belt/pulleys mean a remanufactured or new unit. Preventive: CVT fluid exchange (genuine NS-2/NS-3) every 40,000–60,000 km, and an auxiliary cooler for hot climates or towing
- **Typical cost:** Remanufactured unit installed: USD 3,500–5,500 (US independent shops) / JPY 30–70万円 (Japan, reman vs. new); valve body alone USD 800–1,500. Fluid exchange JPY 2–4万円 / USD 200–400

## Buyer checklist

- Test drive at least 30 minutes including a highway climb; watch for RPM flare, shudder at light throttle, and any whine that tracks speed
- Ask for CVT fluid service records; "lifetime fluid, never changed" on a 100,000 km car is a risk marker
- Have a dealer or capable shop read the CVT fluid deterioration counter before purchase
- On 2003–2010 US cars, check whether a replacement unit was already fitted under the extended warranty (a documented replacement is a plus)
- Budget assumption: an unverified high-mileage example may need a JPY 30万円+ / USD 4,000+ transmission within your ownership

## Sources

Nissan US CVT warranty extension programs (2003–2010 doubling; later 2012–2017 extensions and judder TSBs such as NTB15-084 revisions); extensively documented in Nissan owner communities, class-action filings, and independent transmission shop experience in both the US and Japan. Verify each vehicle individually — coverage and part revisions vary by VIN and market.
