# Nissan SR20DET (S13/S14/S15) — Timing chain guide rattle

**English** | [日本語](./nissan-sr20det-chain-guide-rattle.ja.md)

## Scope

- **Vehicles:** Silvia/180SX S13, Silvia S14/S15, Pulsar GTI-R, Avenir/Bluebird SR20DET variants; naturally aspirated SR20DE shows it too
- **Engines/components:** SR20DET/SR20DE — upper timing chain guide, chain tensioner, single-row timing chain
- **Production window:** All production years (1989–2002); appears with age and mileage rather than a specific batch
- **Markets:** JDM primarily; global via imports

## The issue

The SR20's upper chain guide is a plastic-faced part that wears and embrittles with age and heat. As the chain tensioner also ages, chain slap chews the guide, producing the famous SR "chain rattle" at idle. Worn-through or shattered guides drop plastic fragments into the sump, and severely neglected engines can wear the chain and tensioner to the point of noisy timing scatter — but outright chain failure is rare. The community fix of simply deleting the upper guide during a rebuild is widespread and accepted, since the guide causes more problems than it solves once old.

## Symptoms an owner can detect

- Metallic rattle/tinkling from the front of the engine at warm idle, often quieting slightly as revs rise
- Rattle on cold start for a few seconds (tensioner bleed-down) that grows to constant with wear
- Plastic fragments in the oil pan or on the pickup screen during service
- In advanced neglect: rougher idle from timing scatter, chain contact marks under the valve cover

## Severity

**Annoyance.** The noise itself is mostly cosmetic and enormously common; cost stays low if addressed before fragments and chain wear accumulate. It graduates to a real problem only with long neglect.

## Frequency (honest assessment)

Near-universal on unopened engines at this age — most 150,000 km+ SR20s rattle to some degree. Actual chain/tensioner failure is uncommon; the main real-world consequence is guide debris in the oil and buyers using the noise to negotiate.

## Fix and cost

- **Repair:** Replace the tensioner and either replace or (common practice) delete the upper guide; do chain, guides, and tensioner together if the front cover is off. Clean debris from the sump/pickup while there
- **Typical cost:** Tensioner + upper guide job JPY 3–8万円 at a Japanese shop; full chain kit with front cover off JPY 10–20万円 / USD 800–1,500 US. DIY-friendly for the tensioner/guide on these engines

## Buyer checklist

- Listen at warm idle with the hood open — rattle from the front cover area is the tell; ask whether the tensioner/guide has been done
- Ask when the oil pan was last off and whether plastic debris was found
- Use the noise as a negotiation item, not a walk-away — but confirm it is chain rattle and not rod knock (chain rattle is higher-pitched, front-of-engine, and doesn't load up with rpm)

## Sources

Ubiquitous in Silvia/180SX and SR20 community documentation in Japan and abroad; upper-guide delete during rebuilds is long-standing specialist practice. Verify each engine by listening and history.
